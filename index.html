            PM10

            Causes

            Producing primarily from construction activities, road dust and sand soil.

            Hazards

            Lead to heart disease, atherosclerosis, hardening of the lungs, lung cancer, asthma, bronchitis and other diseases.

            Precaution

            Wear a dust mask outside.

            PM2.5

            Causes

            It mainly comes from the power generation, industrial production, such as vehicle emissions after combustion and emissions in the process of residues, mostly contain heavy metals and other toxic substances.

            Hazards

            The health risks of micro-fine soot particles stem from their ability to penetrate the lungs and pass into the bloodstream. This is the reason they are associated with cardiovascular disease (diseases of the heart and blood circulation) as well as lung disease.

            Precaution

            1.Staying indoors when the weather is haze. 2.Wear a PM2.5 mask outside.

            SO2

            Causes

            Power generation, metallurgy, building materials is the main source of sulfur dioxide.

            Hazards

            Inhaling sulfur dioxide is associated with increased respiratory symptoms and disease, difficulty in breathing, and premature death.

            Precaution

            Firecrackers Use Should Be Decreased.

            NO2

            Causes

            Thermally produced NOx is the largest contributor to these types of emissions. Thermal NOx is produced during the combustion process when nitrogen and oxygen are present at elevated temperatures.

            Hazards

            . As one of the components of smog, nitrogen dioxide is known to irritate the lungs and increase susceptibility to respiratory infections.

            Precaution

            Avoid opening the window.

            O3

            Causes

            Ozone precursors are a group of pollutants, predominantly those emitted during the combustion of fossil fuels and break down in the lower atmosphere to normal dioxygen.

            Hazards

            Ozone is a powerful oxidant and has many industrial and consumer applications related to oxidation. This same high oxidising potential, however, causes ozone to damage mucous and respiratory tissues in animals, and also tissues in plants. This makes ozone a potent respiratory hazard and pollutant near ground level.

            Precaution

            Avoid opening the window.

            AQI

            AQI

            An air quality index (AQI) is a number used by government agencies to communicate to the public how polluted the air currently is or how polluted it is forecast to become. As the AQI increases, an increasingly large percentage of the population is likely to experience increasingly severe adverse health effects.
